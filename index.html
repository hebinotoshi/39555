Marie Curie

Marie Curie was one of the greatest scientists in history. She was born in Warsaw, Poland, in 1867. When she was young, her family did not have much money, but her parents always encouraged her to study hard. Curie loved reading and learning, and she was very good at science and mathematics at school.

In 1891, Curie moved to Paris, France, to study at a famous university. At that time, it was very unusual for women to study science at university. She worked extremely hard and finished her studies at the top of her class. After university, she began doing research* with her husband, Pierre Curie. Together, they discovered two new chemical elements,* polonium and radium, in 1898.

In 1903, Marie and Pierre Curie won the Nobel Prize* in Physics for their research. Marie became the first woman ever to win a Nobel Prize. Sadly, Pierre died in an accident in 1906. Marie continued her research alone and won a second Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911. She became the first person in history to win Nobel Prizes in two different subjects.

Curie not only worked in her laboratory but also helped people during World War One.* She created small X-ray machines that doctors could use near the battlefield to help injured soldiers. Because of her work, many lives were saved. She continued working until she died in 1934. Today, people around the world remember her as a symbol of hard work, courage, and the power of science.
